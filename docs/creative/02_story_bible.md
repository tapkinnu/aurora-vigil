## I. Core Premise

Years ago, an atmospheric anomaly called **The Shimmer Event** bathed the coast near Meridian City in unstable aurora radiation. Most civilians experienced only vivid skyward lights. A few were changed at the cellular level — their bodies now generate and store ionizing light-energy. One of them chose to stand between the city and the chaos that followed.

That guardian is **The Lumen**.

The Lumen is not an alien. Not a god. Not a soldier. They are a Meridian citizen who woke up after the Shimmer with power they barely understand, in a city that is still arguing over what the aurora even is. Their heroism is a practice, not a birthright — every flight, every rescue, every impossible save is a choice made under pressure.

## II. Protagonist Identity

**Civilian Name:** Not fixed — player may choose from a shortlist at character creation:
- **Kael Maren** — infrastructure engineer, works on aurora collector maintenance
- **Sable Acheson** — paramedic, first-responder background
- **Davi Orin** — transit operator on the Meridian sky-rail network

**Hero Name:** The Lumen (widely known in media and emergency channels)

**Armor Description:**
- Form-fitting base layer of matte black weave with teal bioluminescent circuit traces
- Gold ion-mantle fins along the forearms, spine, and calves — these glow brighter with power use
- No cape. No letter emblem. No mask that covers the full face.
- Half-visor HUD in teal, leaving the lower face visible — the public sees a person, not a symbol
- Contrail signature: warm gold with a faint teal edge, unique and trackable

**Vocal Personality:**
- Calm under pressure, but never robotic
- Speaks to civilians in second person ("Stay with me, you're okay")
- Dry humor when the situation allows, but never at someone's expense
- Refers to powers functionally, not poetically ("Shield up" not "By the light!")

## III. The Shimmer Event (Backstory)

**Date:** Approximately 7 years before game start.

**What happened:** A classified orbital weather experiment (Project Halo) destabilized above the Meridian coast. The resulting energy discharge interacted with the upper atmosphere for 40 seconds. Auroral light was visible across three states. No deaths, but 12,000 civilians reported "persistent light sensitivity" in follow-up health screenings.

**What actually happened (known only to late-game story):** Project Halo was an attempt to create a clean energy relay using ionized particulates. It succeeded — then exceeded safe parameters by a factor of 200. The aurora collectors around Meridian City were built afterward in part to absorb residual atmospheric charge, and in part by interests who knew the Shimmer would recur.

**The Lumen's origin:** Exposed during the event while performing a civilian act of bravery (context varies by chosen backstory). The ionizing charge bonded with their biology during a moment of selfless action — not a guaranteed outcome, not a prophecy, just a body responding unusually to extreme conditions.

## IV. Setting: Meridian City

**Type:** Near-future coastal vertical city, population ~3.2 million  
**Geography:** Built on a series of tiered cliff-platforms rising from a dark, storm-prone ocean. The poorest districts are at sea level; the wealthiest crest the upper tiers.

### Key Locations

| Location | Description | Gameplay Role |
|---|---|---|
| **The Shelf** | Lowest residential tier, built into the cliff face, prone to flooding and drone incursions | Tutorial district, early rescue events |
| **Conduit Row** | Mid-level commercial strip built around the main aurora collector spire | Hub for civilian interactions, bark radio chatter |
| **Halo Spire** | Tallest structure, houses the primary aurora collector array and Civic Grid core | Late-game mission focal point |
| **The Drift** | Floating market district suspended between upper platforms by transit tethers | Navigation challenge, chase sequences |
| **Stormwall Barrier** | Massive seawall with embedded ion dampeners, showing wear | Disaster event site, Shimmer-related anomalies |
| **Foundry Quarter** | Industrial zone where aurora tech is manufactured | Enemy ambush zone, faction conflict |
| **Resonance Park** | Green space built around a Shimmer memorial, popular with families | Calm contrast zone, civilian trust events |
| **Blackglass Row** | Upper-tier corporate enclave, gleaming and surveilled | Moral complexity zone, not everyone here is a villain |

### City Mood

Meridian City feels alive, contested, and layered. The rich have height and clean air. The poor have history and resilience. Aurora energy flows through the infrastructure like a circulatory system — beautiful, useful, and potentially dangerous if hijacked. Transit lines stitch the vertical city together, and The Lumen's gold contrail across the sky becomes a symbol of connection: someone who goes everywhere, for everyone.

## V. Themes

1. **Power and Responsibility as Daily Practice:** The Lumen doesn't get a choice about having powers, but every use of those powers is a choice. The game asks: what do you protect, and what do you sacrifice?

2. **Infrastructure is Civic Trust:** The aurora collectors, the drone grid, the transit system — when they work, the city works. When they fail, people suffer. The Lumen protects infrastructure not for its own sake, but because people depend on it.

3. **Radical Transparency vs. Secret Identities:** The Lumen operates openly. There is no hidden civilian disguise. The tension isn't "will they be discovered?" — it's "what does it cost to be seen at all times?"

4. **Trauma and Recovery:** The Shimmer Event left scars — physical, psychological, political. The Storm Children (civilians changed by the event) range from grateful to angry. The city's healing is incomplete, and The Lumen is both a symbol of hope and a walking reminder of what happened.

5. **Systems, Not Supervillains:** Most threats in Meridian City are systemic — corruption, negligence, fear, exploitation. The factions are not purely evil; they have reasons. The Lumen's hardest choices aren't about punching harder — they're about which broken system to prioritize.

## VI. Power Progression Arc

| Tier | Powers Unlocked | Narrative Stage |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** (Levels 1-3) | Flight, Boost, Basic Scan | Learning phase — player understands controls, city layout, civilian rescue basics |
| **Tier 2** (Levels 4-6) | Radiant Beam, Sonic Burst | Confidence phase — harder events, ability to clear debris and disable small tech threats |
| **Tier 3** (Levels 7-9) | Aegis Field, Rescue Lift | Protector phase — structured missions, shielding civilians from disasters, carrying wounded |
| **Tier 4** (Levels 10-12) | Orbit Sprint, Pulse Wave | Guardian phase — citywide emergencies, rapid long-distance response, late-game story missions |
| **Tier 5** (Level 13+) | Aurora Convergence (ultimate) — temporary city-scale protective dome | Endgame phase — addressing the biggest threat, full power realization |

Power use drains a shared **Ion Meter** that recharges in sunlight and near aurora collectors — creating risk/replay loops around collector locations and time of day.

## VII. Tone & Content Guidance

**On-screen consequences:** When The Lumen fails to protect someone, the game shows it — emergency responders arrive, civilians express distress, city metrics drop. This is not graphic; it is emotional weight.

**No killing.** The Lumen does not kill. Enemies are disabled, contained, or redirected. The moral complexity comes from triage decisions, not body counts.

**Civilian trust as currency:** The city's trust in The Lumen is a visible metric. It rises with successful rescues and falls with collateral damage or mission failure. Low trust changes NPC dialogue and radio chatter — not game-over, but a different emotional texture.

**Hope as default.** Even in crisis, the game's tone trends toward hope. The Lumen is not a dark, brooding figure. They are tired sometimes, scared sometimes, but they keep showing up.

## VIII. Anti-Copy Policy (Legal Safety)

This section exists to document what was deliberately avoided. The broad archetype of a flying, super-strong protector is a public-domain storytelling template. The following are **banned** from all Aurora Vigil creative output:

- **Names:** Superman, Clark Kent, Kal-El, Lois Lane, Lex Luthor, Perry White, Jimmy Olsen, Jor-El, Jonathan Kent, Martha Kent, Zod, Doomsday, Brainiac, Darkseid, Bizarro, Metallo, Parasite, Manchester Black, or any DC Comics character name.
- **Symbols:** S-shield, any inverted-triangle emblem, Kryptonian script, House of El iconography.
- **Costume elements:** Red cape, red boots, blue tights, yellow belt with red borders, red trunks over blue, any chest emblem shaped like a shield or diamond.
- **Locations:** Metropolis, Smallville, Fortress of Solitude, Daily Planet, LexCorp, Wayne Enterprises, Gotham, Arkham.
- **Story beats:** Alien origin from a destroyed planet, raised by rural adoptive parents who find him in a ship, weakness to green crystalline substance from his home world, love interest who works at a newspaper, archenemy who is a bald corporate mogul.
- **Power framing:** "Man of Steel," "Last Son," "Truth, Justice, and the American Way," "Faster than a speeding bullet."

If any banned term appears elsewhere in this document or its siblings, it is an error and must be reported and corrected.

## IX. Narrative Seasons (Post-Launch Roadmap)

**Season 1 — Ignition (Launch):** The Lumen's first months. Establishing the city, core factions, and the mystery of why the Shimmer's residual energy is increasing.

**Season 2 — Fracture:** The Null Choir escalates. A second Shimmer event is predicted. The Lumen must choose between protecting the city's infrastructure or exposing the truth about Project Halo.

**Season 3 — Convergence:** The aurora collectors reach critical mass. The Storm Children's abilities evolve. The Lumen must unite the factions or watch Meridian tear itself apart.

Each season adds districts, powers, events, and missions. The story bible is designed to scale.
